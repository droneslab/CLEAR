Department of Computer Science and Engineering, University at Buffalo

CLEAR Paper Videos

Large-scale off-road planning requires repeated route queries over terrain maps spanning 9--100 km2. CLEAR builds a reusable semantic-geometric terrain graph, while HOPHY uses hierarchy and hypergraphs to support fast repeated queries, local condition updates, and multi-agent mission planning.

Abstract

Long-horizon navigation in unstructured environments demands terrain abstractions that scale to tens of square kilometers while preserving semantic and geometric structure. Existing grids scale poorly, while quadtrees can misalign with terrain boundaries and omit landcover semantics that matter for traversability-aware planning.

CLEAR addresses the representation problem by constructing a reusable terrain abstraction from landcover and elevation. It produces convex, semantically aligned regions encoded as a terrain-aware graph. HOPHY builds on this abstraction for mission-scale planning: it compresses map-scale terrain into geometric-semantic nodes and coarse regions, then uses typed hyperedges to update terrain constraints under changing weather or agent-specific costs.

Across kilometer-scale maps, CLEAR reduces per-query planning time while preserving executability in physics-based simulation. HOPHY further reduces query time to the millisecond regime, supports localized replanning, and enables fast multi-agent path-cost estimation for large off-road missions.

CLEAR: Terrain Abstraction

CLEAR operates through Boundary-Seed Decomposition, recursive plane fitting, and graph encoding. Boundary-Seed Decomposition places seeds in flat regions and along high-entropy semantic boundaries, then uses Voronoi partitioning to form compact convex regions. Plane fitting approximates elevation within each region and recursively subdivides regions whose error exceeds a tolerance. The final abstraction stores landcover, elevation geometry, convex boundaries, and adjacency for graph search.

CLEAR pipeline: landcover and elevation input, Boundary-Seed Decomposition, recursive planar surface fitting, and terrain-aware graph encoding.
Compared with grid, hexagonal, and quadtree abstractions, CLEAR explicitly targets terrain boundaries and semantic-geometric consistency.

HOPHY: Hierarchical Hypergraph Planning

HOPHY targets mission-level settings where path planning is an inner-loop primitive for task allocation, course-of-action comparison, and replanning after terrain changes. Pixel-level planners are accurate but too slow for many repeated queries, while flat abstractions do not provide the hierarchy and localized updates needed for dynamic missions.

HOPHY represents terrain with GSNodes and coarse regions. GSNodes aggregate connected terrain with consistent landcover and elevation or grade statistics, and coarse regions group GSNodes for large-scale reasoning. Typed hyperedges encode terrain and contextual constraints such as landcover, grade, weather, and agent feasibility. When conditions change, HOPHY filters affected GSNode sets instead of recomputing the full graph.

HOPHY hierarchy and planning framework: terrain inputs are abstracted into Fine Regions, GSNodes, coarse regions, and typed hyperedges for fast multi-agent path planning.

Results

CLEAR is evaluated on 9--100 km2 digital terrain maps with physics-based simulation. It reduces per-query planning time over unabstracted raw-grid A* after one-time abstraction and achieves 100% task completion across the evaluated maps in simulation.

CLEAR planning and BeamNG simulation results on Wharton, Humphrey, and Rainier terrain maps.

HOPHY keeps median cost delta at 0.00% relative to pixel-level A* on Wharton, Humphrey, and Rainier, while reducing median query time by 68x--1280x in the paper results. Under rain and localized blockage updates, HOPHY maintains near-A* path quality with sub-186 ms mean query time across all maps.

HOPHY remains in the millisecond query-time regime while preserving path quality close to the pixel-level reference.
Dynamic replanning and multi-agent mission planning: typed hyperedges support local updates, and the maintained hierarchy enables fast path-cost estimation for heterogeneous teams.
